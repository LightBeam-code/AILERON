THE HISTORY OF AEROPLANES

The earliest concept of a plane actually dates back to 1490 rather than the well acknowledged year of 1905. Back then, Leonardo da Vinci> made a piece of art depicting his plan on a 'one-seater' machine that would be used to fly from place to place, that being, the Leonardo da Vinci flying machine. Centuries later,on the evening of September 18,1901 ,Wilbur Wright addressed a respected group of Chicago engineers on 'some aeronautical experiments' that he and his brother, Orville Wright did over the past 2 years. He realised that from the experiments,there were challenges that thus needed to be conquered. Later in 1905, the Wright brothers> successfully tackled the 'prototype plane''s problems and made the Wright Flyer, the VERY FIRST PLANE !

Since then, more research into aerodynamics has led to the evolution of modern planes. For example, in 1853, George Cayley's glider was invented. Afterwards the Lilienthal glider was made in the 1870s, the Langley aerodome in 1903 and many more inventions that led to the modern plane that we have today.
